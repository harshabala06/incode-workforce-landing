The Problem

Standard IAM doesn't verify people.
It verifies credentials.

Passwords, tokens, and knowledge-based checks confirm what someone has or knows — not who they actually are. That gap is exactly what attackers exploit.

🎭
Fraudulent Hires
Imposters use stolen or AI-enhanced identities to pass background checks. KnowBe4 hired a North Korean IT worker using a fabricated identity — and only discovered it after malware was deployed on day one.
🤖
Deepfake Social Engineering
Help desk agents and IT teams are the most common entry point for account takeovers. A convincing phone call or video impersonation is all it takes to trigger a password reset for a bad actor.
🔑
Credential-Based Recovery Gaps
Most MFA reset and account recovery flows validate only credentials, not the person holding them. Stolen device tokens or SIM-swapped numbers give attackers a clear path to privileged access.
How It Works

One verified identity.
Every touchpoint.

Incode enrolls a biometric identity once — then reuses it silently at every moment that matters, with no re-upload or friction for employees.

01
Enrollment at First Touchpoint
A candidate completes a guided ID + selfie check once — through Workday, Greenhouse, or a direct link. Incode creates a permanent biometric record. No re-upload ever required.
02
Selfie-Only at Every Step After
Every subsequent verification — Okta activation, MFA enrollment, help desk request, privileged access — requires only a quick selfie matched against the original biometric.
03
Passive Liveness + Deepfake Detection
Multi-modal passive liveness analyzes micro-expressions, lighting, and motion. Integrated deepfake and injection detection stops synthetic attacks before they reach your systems.
04
Works Inside Your Existing Stack
No new portals or apps for employees. Verification triggers natively inside Okta, Entra, Workday, ServiceNow, and Slack — where your teams already live.
